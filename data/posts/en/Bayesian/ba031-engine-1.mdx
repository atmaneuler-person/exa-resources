## Decision Impedance Matching

Through the previous installments of [BA02. Bayesian Inference](https://news.exavions.com/en/ba02-exa-bayesian-sales-nsbi/), we explored how the Bayesian engine tracks probability trajectories. Now, we hold in our hands the pure posterior probability [latex]P\_{raw}[/latex] calculated by parameters [latex]\alpha[/latex] and [latex]\beta[/latex].

---

## 1. The Pitfall of Numbers: Why 51% is Not Enough

Mathematically, 51% means 'success is more likely.' However, in the life-or-death arena of business, 51% is practically a gamble.

- **Situation A:** $$

\alpha=0.6, \beta=0.4 \implies P = 60\%

$$


- **Situation B:**
$$

\alpha=60, \beta=40 \implies P = 60\%

$$


---


## 2. Total Amount of Evidence ( n ): The Critical Mass

The Exa engine uses the Bayesian posterior probability ( [latex]P_{raw}[/latex] ) and the total amount of evidence ( [latex]n = \alpha + \beta[/latex] ) as a decision-making filter.


---


## 3. The Hidden World: Accumulation of Log-odds

Inside the engine, probability does not look like the 0â€“100% we know. Parameters [latex]\alpha[/latex] and [latex]\beta[/latex] operate in a linear space called **Log-odds**.


$$

logit(P) = \ln\left(\frac{P}{1-P}\right) = \ln\left(\frac{\alpha}{\beta}\right)

$$


Every negotiation signal adds to this value:
- Success signals push the value up via [latex]\Delta \alpha[/latex].
- Failure signals and time decay ([latex]\lambda[/latex]) pull it down via [latex]\Delta \beta[/latex].


---


## 5. Business Simulation

Final Formula:


$$

P\_{out} = \frac{1}{1 + \left( \frac{\beta \cdot T}{\alpha(1-T)} \right)^k}

$$


### 5.1 Business Scenario Simulation

#### Situation 1: Below Threshold (Uncertain State)

- **Data:** [latex]\alpha = 6.0, \beta = 4.0[/latex] (Pure Probability [latex]P_{raw} = 60\%[/latex])

#### Calculation Process:

#### Ratio Calculation:


$$

\frac{4.0 \cdot 0.7}{6.0 \cdot 0.3} = \frac{2.8}{1.8} \approx 1.55

$$


#### Applying power of k:


$$

1.55^{10} \approx 81.3

$$


- **Final:** [latex]P_{out} = \frac{1}{1 + 81.3} \approx[/latex] **1.2%**


#### Situation 2: Breakthrough (Start of Decisiveness)

- **Data:** [latex]\alpha = 7.2, \beta = 2.8[/latex] (Pure Probability [latex]P_{raw} = 72\%[/latex])
- **Final Probability:** [latex]P_{out} = \frac{1}{1 + 0.38} \approx[/latex] **72.4%**


#### Situation 3: Critical Mass (Commitment Phase)

- **Data:** [latex]\alpha = 8.5, \beta = 1.5[/latex] (Pure Probability [latex]P_{raw} = 85\%[/latex])
- **Final Probability:** [latex]P_{out} = \frac{1}{1 + 0.0001} \approx[/latex] **99.9%**
